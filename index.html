About Me

I am a developmental psychologist at the University of Tokyo, specializing in child development, attachment theory, and family systems. My research focuses on understanding how diverse family structures influence children's social-emotional development.

I aim to reconstruct a resilient modern allomothering system by reimagining the social ecosystem of parenting through an interdisciplinary lens.

My work spans from evolutionary models of allomothering in Mosuo matrilineal society (research prototype), to qualitative analysis of clinical pain points in modern isolated parenting (needs diagnosis), to quantitative validation through large-scale longitudinal data (empirical support). By integrating AI affective computing technology, I am committed to translating complex developmental psychology mechanisms into accessible, tangible modern parenting support tools—building a new paradigm of "assisted caregiving" where technology and humanity interweave.

Evolutionary Blueprint

Deep fieldwork in matrilineal society as a "natural experiment," uncovering resilience mechanisms in human allomothering that transcend cultural boundaries.

Clinical Pathology

Identifying mental health breaking points caused by contemporary "isolated parenting" in modern urban nuclear families (ACEs, maternal anxiety, etc.).

Longitudinal Validation

Participating in Japan's large-scale longitudinal studies to validate the universality and specificity of these mechanisms in the Japanese social context, establishing scientific basis for policy intervention.

Modern Solutions

Transforming insights into accessible technological tools, achieving a closed loop from "theory" to "clinical practice" to "large-scale social implementation."

As a registered psychologist (CCPRS) trained in psychoanalytic and psychodynamic psychotherapy, my clinical practice focuses on family-of-origin dynamics, personal growth, and the intricacies of interpersonal and emotional relationships.

Research Interests

Matrilineal Family Systems

Investigating the dual family relationship system and allomothering practices among the Mosuo people, examining how non-nuclear family structures support child development.

Attachment Theory

Exploring non-hierarchical attachment models in extended families, including children's relationships with mothers, aunts, grandmothers, and other caregivers.

Social-Emotional Development

Examining how different family structures and caregiver qualities influence children's cognitive emotion regulation and social development.

Mindful Parenting

Researching the effects of maternal mindful parenting on child development and the implementation of CBT interventions for adolescents and parents.

AI & Emotional Development

Designing AI systems that assess attachment security and provide developmentally appropriate emotional support across cultural contexts, exploring how technology can enhance rather than replace human caregiving.
